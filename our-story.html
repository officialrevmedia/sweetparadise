A family story, baked into Hamilton.

In 1986, Carmen and Concentina Mancini opened a small Italian bakery on Hamilton's mountain with their three sons. The bread came out hot. The line started forming. It hasn't really stopped since.

Carmen Mancini came to Canada with his recipes and his wife.

He opened Sweet Paradise in 1986. Three sons grew up in it. They still run it.

Concentina and the late Carmen Mancini both came from the Castelli region of Italy, where they farmed the land and dreamed of a life in Canada. Carmen always imagined opening a bakery of his own. When he first arrived he helped establish a well-known Hamilton business, Carmen's, but the dream of a traditional Italian bakery never left him. With Concentina and their three children, Pat, Dino, and Lidio, he opened the doors of Sweet Paradise in 1986.

Carmen was renowned for his Calabrese bread and his love of beekeeping. Dino went on to study at George Brown College in Toronto to become a certified baking chef, learning from professionals from around the world before returning home to spend the next thirty years mastering the craft. Together the family built the bedrock of a traditional Italian bakery, carrying more than sixty years of baking between them.

We are still famous for our hearth-baked style and original family recipes, baked fresh daily from scratch with no preservatives or additives. The pasta sauce recipe traces back to 1925. The cake sponges are Dino's. The way we treat customers like family is Carmen's. Over the years that work has been recognized by our community, including the Hamilton Spectator Readers' Choice Award.

Today, Sweet Paradise has two locations, a thirty-foot deli case, a forty-foot freezer of prepared meals, a catering operation that handles events of five hundred guests, and a Hamilton community that has made us part of birthdays, holidays, baptisms, and Sunday dinners for generations.

We invite you to be a part of our family, and to help us continue creating a little slice of paradise.

The Story, In Short

A timeline.

1986

Sweet Paradise opens

Carmen Mancini opens a small Italian bakery on Stone Church Road, Hamilton.

1990s

Deli & catering arrive

The deli counter opens. Catering becomes a major part of the business as Hamilton families and businesses keep calling.

2000s

Pat, Dino & Lidio step in

The next generation takes the lead. Dino's cake program grows into the signature program it is today.

2010s

Waterdown location opens

A second store on Dundas Street E. brings Sweet Paradise to a new community.

Today

A Hamilton staple

Two locations serving bread, cakes, pastries, deli, catering, prepared meals, and Italian grocery. Over 100 years of combined baking experience in the kitchen.
